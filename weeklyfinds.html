Audi · Certified Pre-Owned

2023 A6 allroad
Prestige

One of seven in the nation. The only Certified Prestige trim available.

Listed At
$61,945
⭐ Certified Pre-Owned · 1 of 7 in the U.S.

There are exactly seven 2023 Audi A6 allroads with this spec available in the entire United States right now — and this is the only one that's both Certified and in the top-tier Prestige trim. Every other example you'll find listed nationally is a Premium Plus. That's not dealer talk. That's a real number in a genuinely scarce car.

The A6 allroad occupies a rare lane: it's a lifted station wagon with the heart of a performance car — a 3.0-liter turbocharged V6 producing 335 horsepower and 369 pound-feet of torque, all routed through quattro all-wheel drive and a seven-speed automatic. Audi says 0-60 in 5.1 seconds. Real-world drivers have gone faster. What the numbers don't capture is how effortlessly it does it — there's no drama, no noise, just propulsion.

"The Prestige trim adds everything worth having — adaptive air suspension, night vision, Bang & Olufsen, the works. The Premium Plus is a fine car. This one is something different."

The Prestige trim piles on top of an already well-equipped base: adaptive air suspension that can raise ground clearance for light off-road duty, night vision with thermal imaging, a Bang & Olufsen 3D audio system, 18-way massage seats, Audi's virtual cockpit with heads-up display, and a black suede headliner that makes the cabin feel genuinely premium. The CPO certification adds Audi's factory warranty coverage — no guessing on what you're buying.

For the person who wants something that does it all — daily driver comfort, weekend gravel road capability, luxury interior, and a touch of rarity — this might be the most complete vehicle in this week's issue.

Find No. 02
Audi Sport · Brand New 2026

2026 RS3 Sedan

Five cylinders. 394 horsepower. The world's most entertaining compact sedan.

Listed At
$76,485
✦ Brand New · 2026 Model Year

No car at this price point makes a sound like the RS3. The turbocharged 2.5-liter inline five-cylinder — 394 horsepower, 369 pound-feet of torque — has a firing order so unusual it produces a bark and burble on lift-off that Audi has spent years refining into an art form. BMW's M2 has power. The Cadillac CT4-V Blackwing has drama. The RS3 has soul.

For 2026 the RS3 carries over its recent refresh largely unchanged — which is fine, because what Audi refreshed two years ago was already the most complete version of this car. New standard equipment includes Matrix-design LED headlights with customizable DRL signatures and welcome/leaving animations. The 12.3-inch Virtual Cockpit Plus comes with RS-specific display themes — Runway and Performance — that show real-time torque vectoring and boost pressure readings.

"394 horsepower in a compact sedan with a five-cylinder that sounds like nothing else on the road. At $76K, you're not buying a car. You're buying an experience."

The RS-specific torque-vectoring quattro system biases power rearward, giving the RS3 a playfulness that quattro usually suppresses. Optional Dynamic Plus package adds ceramic brakes and a coilover suspension. The standard package already includes RS-tuned steering, launch control, and a sport exhaust with black titanium tips that does not disappoint. This is a new car, so no Carfax — but that's the point.

BMW · M Sport Package

2026 X5 M Sport

22-inch wheels, M Sport Pro, white over black. Nearly new at a used price.

Listed At
$66,981
✦ 2026 Model · Nearly New · Clean Carfax

A 2026 BMW X5 at under $67,000 — on a car whose new MSRP starts around $68,000 and climbs fast once you add packages — is worth a closer look. This one left the factory with M Sport, M Sport Professional, Climate Comfort, and a Live Cockpit Pro that accounts for a significant portion of what made it expensive when ordered. Now it's used, it's nearly new, and it's meaningfully below what a similarly-equipped new unit costs today.

The X5 sDrive40i powertrain is the 3.0-liter turbocharged inline-six producing 375 horsepower — a unit that BMW has refined over the better part of a decade into one of the smoothest, most responsive engines in the luxury SUV segment. It's not the all-wheel-drive xDrive setup, but sDrive in a BMW still means a near-50/50 weight distribution and a handling balance that SUV buyers typically can't find elsewhere.

"22-inch wheels on an X5 with M Sport Pro packaging in white over black is the spec most people build. This one's already built — and sitting below new price."

White exterior with black interior is consistently the easiest X5 combination to sell when the time comes. The M Sport Professional package adds the blacked-out exterior trim, the sportier front fascia, and the kind of exterior detail that puts clear distance between this car and a standard X5 in the rearview. Carfax shows no accidents. 22,601 miles on a 2026 model — this was clearly someone's test of the market, not a car that was driven hard.

Find No. 04
Chevrolet · Z06 · 3LZ

2024 Corvette Z06

670 naturally aspirated horsepower. 8,600 rpm redline. Torch Red. 1,950 miles.

Listed At
$115,960
✦ 1,950 Miles · Top Spec 3LZ · Clean Carfax

The LT6 engine in the C8 Z06 is not a conventional performance car engine. It is a hand-built, naturally aspirated 5.5-liter flat-plane V8 that revs to 8,600 rpm and produces 670 horsepower without forced induction. In production car terms, that is a Ferrari-level engineering achievement at roughly one-quarter of Ferrari's price. The sound it makes at full throttle is unlike anything else wearing an American badge.

This example — 3LZ, the top Z06 trim — adds everything the car offers: GT2 Napa leather seats with carbon-fiber trim, Performance Data Recorder, front lift system, the full carbon aerodynamics package, head-up display, navigation, and Bose Performance audio. Torch Red over Natural/Napa leather is a classic American sports car combination. 1,950 miles means this car has barely been unwrapped.

"The LT6 is one of the greatest naturally aspirated engines ever installed in a production car. At under $116K with 1,950 miles, this is the closest thing to a new Z06 without new-car pricing."

Carfax is clean. The mid-engine layout gives the Z06 a weight distribution and grip level that embarrasses significantly more expensive machinery from Europe. The 8-speed dual-clutch transmission manages the power delivery without drama, but drop it into manual mode and it becomes a completely different conversation. This is an experience car, not just a fast car.

Find No. 05
Lamborghini · Super SUV

2022 Urus

The original Super SUV. Texas-owned. No accidents. Style Package. $199,995.

Listed At
$199,995
✦ Texas-Owned · No Accidents · Style Package

Lamborghini built the Urus to prove a point — that a Sant'Agata SUV could still be a Lamborghini in every meaningful way. Four-liter twin-turbocharged V8, 641 horsepower, 627 pound-feet of torque, zero to 60 in 3.5 seconds. The numbers are real. The experience backs them up. And unlike some exotic claims, the Urus actually delivers those figures in the real world, in traffic, in Texas heat.

This example was Texas-owned from purchase — Carfax shows a clean title, no accidents, and 9 service records. Acquired in June 2022, it was maintained locally in Houston and has racked up honest miles without incident. The Style Package adds visual differentiation with sport-specific exterior trim, upgraded alloy wheel design, and rear spoiler treatment that makes the Urus look distinctly more aggressive than the standard spec.

"The Urus doesn't ask you to choose between practicality and theater. It is both, simultaneously, without apology."

Inside, the Urus offers a Lamborghini experience without the Lamborghini compromises — full-size rear seats, proper luggage space, a sensible infotainment system borrowed (and improved) from the Audi/Porsche parts bin. The driving modes range from genuinely comfortable GT mode on the highway to a Corsa setting that stiffens the suspension and opens the exhaust to a level that will disturb neighbors. It's a ridiculous, wonderful machine.

At $199,995, this sits noticeably below recent transactions for comparably-specced 2022 Urus examples with similar mileage. The clean Texas history and service documentation make this one of the cleaner examples you'll find at this price point.

Mercedes-AMG · G-Class · Manufaktur

2024 AMG G63
Green Hell
Magno

Manufaktur Green Hell Magno over Lime Green interior. The loudest statement in this issue.

Listed At

🟢 Manufaktur · Green Hell Magno · 1-of-Few Spec

Green Hell Magno is not a paint option you check on a configurator and move on. It is a Manufaktur finish — matte, hand-applied, requiring specific care, and specific attention from the people around you. Named after the Nürburgring Nordschleife, the color carries that reference seriously. Against the black exterior trim of the refreshed G63, it is otherworldly.

This 2024 example — the first year of the significantly refreshed G-Class generation — pairs the Manufaktur exterior with a Black with Lime Green leather interior. That interior combination exists in a very specific intersection of "unexpected" and "correct." The 2024 AMG G63 added meaningful engineering improvements to the formula: the familiar twin-turbocharged 4.0-liter V8 now makes 577 horsepower with a 48-volt mild-hybrid system providing an additional 21 horsepower on demand. New launch control. New driver assistance suite. A revised front fascia that updates the silhouette while respecting 45 years of design heritage.

"The G63 has never been about restraint. Green Hell Magno over Lime Green interior is the version of this car that commits fully to that philosophy."

Zero to 60 in 4.4 seconds. Three locking differentials. Low-range gearing. A car that will genuinely go off-road and genuinely embarrass sports cars on the on-ramp. The absurdity is the point, and this spec executes it at the highest possible level. The Carfax is clean. This is not a car that sits on the market long.
